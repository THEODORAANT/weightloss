Common side effects you should be aware of when using Mounjaro and Wegovy:

• Nausea and heartburn, constipation, headache, vomiting, diarrhea, injection site reactions.

These tend to subside within a few hours to a day. If you notice any of these are getting worse, please contact 111 or your GP.

Severe reactions to be aware of that require attendance at A&E or an ambulance (Call 999):

• Swelling of your face, lips, mouth, tongue or throat.
• Trouble breathing, swallowing or talking.
• Severe rash, hives or itching.
• Very rapid heartbeat.
• Wheezing or feeling of chest tightness.
• Fainting or feeling dizzy.
• Blistered or peeling skin (with or without a fever).
• Yellowing of the skin.
• Severe abdominal pain that may also radiate to your back with or without severe nausea and vomiting, and/or fever or pain in the right shoulder.

How to dispose of your needles

• Used needles, injection pens, and full sharps bins are classed as clinical waste in the UK and must not be placed in regular household rubbish. Disposing of them correctly helps protect you, your family, and the wider community from injury and infection.
• After each injection, carefully replace the outer needle cap. Unscrew the needle from the pen and place it directly into your sharps bin.
• Store your sharps bin out of reach of children and pets, and never fill it beyond the marked fill line.

How to dispose your pen
When you have completed your 4 doses, you can place a used pen into your sharps bin.

How to dispose your sharps bin

• When your sharps bin is full, lock it securely and do not try to reopen it.
• Please label your sharps bin clearly with the following information:
- Your Full Name
- Your Full Address
- The Date the Bin was Sealed (the date you stop adding used pens/needles and seal the bin for collection)
• You can take the sharps bin to your local pharmacy (if they accept sharps) or arrange a clinical waste collection from your local council here.
